3 Days / Week 6 Weeks UCS Correction Dumbbells + Bench Boxing Performance

⚠ Upper Cross Syndrome — Postural Priority

Tight — Needs Release

Pecs · Upper Traps · Levator Scap · Suboccipitals

Weak — Needs Work

Deep Neck Flexors · Lower Traps · Rhomboids · Serratus

Solo Training Rule: Neutral grip on all pressing. Retract shoulder blades before every rep. Every exercise in this plan is chosen to be safe if form isn't perfect — no heavy overhead loading, no wide-grip pressing. If shoulder pain increases, skip the pressing and double the rows.

📈 6-Week Progression

Weeks 1–2: 3 sets. Moderate weight, perfect form. Rest 90s.

Weeks 3–4: Add a 4th set. Increase weight by 2.5–5kg where form is solid. Rest 75s.

Weeks 5–6: Increase load again or add 1–2 reps per set. Rest 60s. Add a 4th round to circuits.

Never increase both weight and volume in the same week. Shoulder sore — keep weight the same, add reps only.

Correctives 8 min · Every Session

Chin Tucks
Forward HeadHeadaches
3 × 15
Draw chin straight back — not down. Hold 3 seconds. Eyes level throughout. Do this before every session, every day without exception.

Thread the Needle
Thoracic MobRotation
2 × 8 each
On all fours. Slide one arm under your body along the floor, rotating your upper back until that shoulder touches the mat. Hold 2 seconds, feel the thoracic stretch. Return and repeat. Hips stay square throughout.
↳ This is purely thoracic — hips don't move, no shoulder load.
🪽
Wall Angels
Lower TrapSerratus
3 × 12
Full back, head, and arms flat against the wall. Goalpost arms. Slide up overhead keeping everything in contact. Slow tempo — if the back peels away, work within your current range.
🔁
Prone Y-T-W
Lower TrapRhomboids
3 × 10
Face down on bench. Very light dumbbells (2–4kg max). Thumbs up. Y = diagonal overhead. T = straight out to sides. W = elbows bent 90°, forearms rotate upward.
↳ Burning in mid-back = right. Burning in neck = too heavy, reduce load.

Chest-Supported DB Row

3–4 × 10
Face down on incline bench. Retract shoulder blades first — then row. Squeeze 1 second at top. 3-count lower. Chest supported means zero lower back or shoulder compensation risk.

Lat · BackCore
3–4 × 10
Knee and hand on bench. Elbow drives back to hip — not wide. No rotation. Squeeze hard at top. Elbow to back pocket, not shoulder height.

DB Floor Press — Neutral Grip
Chest · TricepShoulder-Safe
3–4 × 10
Lie on the floor, knees bent, DBs in neutral grip. Lower until elbows touch the floor — this is the natural stop point and prevents any shoulder over-extension. Press back up. Elbows 45° from torso.
↳ The floor acts as a built-in depth limiter. Even if form slips, shoulders stay safe.
🔄
Lying DB External Rotation
Rotator CuffShoulder Health
3 × 15
Lie on side. Elbow pinned to ribs, bent 90°. Rotate forearm upward. Very light — 2–5kg only. Elbow must not lift away from body.
↳ Critical for boxing shoulder longevity. Never skip this.
🦾

Thumbs slightly up. Raise to shoulder height only — no higher. 3-count lower. Shoulders stay depressed throughout — no shrugging to assist.
